# Adventure Background

## The Core Conflict

The **Whispering Way** seeks to transform Golarion into a world ruled entirely by undead. While this vision appeals to many undead creatures, **most vampires reject it** for a practical reason: they still depend on the blood of the living. If every mortal were turned into an undead creature, vampires would eventually have nothing left to feed upon.

Following **Tar-Baphon's** defeat during the **Shining Crusade**, the vampire clans abandoned the Whispering Tyrant rather than attempting to free him. They retreated into the shadows of **Ustalav**, establishing their own courts and remaining independent for centuries.

---

# Adivion Adrissant's Conspiracy

While researching ways to resurrect **Tar-Baphon**, **Adivion Adrissant** uncovered this ancient betrayal. Realizing the old vampire nobility would almost certainly oppose the Whispering Way, he devised a long-term plan:

- Eliminate the old vampire leadership.
- Replace them with younger vampires loyal to the Whispering Way.
- Build an undead army before Tar-Baphon's return.

To accomplish this, he needed:
- An agent within vampire society.
- A method to break elder vampires' control over their spawn.
- Enough chaos to destabilize Caliphas's vampire hierarchy.

---

# The Twin Witches

Adivion recruited the twin witches **Aisa and Hetna Dublesse**, promising them **lichdom** in exchange for their service.

The sisters had already enslaved an undead **Blood Knight**, whose endlessly bleeding blood became the key ingredient in their greatest creation.

## Bloodbrew

**Bloodbrew** is an addictive blood-based elixir that temporarily suppresses the supernatural domination elder vampires exert over their spawn.

This gave Adivion the perfect tool to create dissent within vampire society.

---

# Radvir Giovanni

Adivion's next recruit was **Radvir Giovanni**, a relatively minor vampire and tailor who lived under the absolute control of his ancient master, **Luvick Siervage**.

After drinking Bloodbrew, Radvir briefly experienced true free will for the first time.

Adivion promised him:

- Freedom from Luvick.
- Political power.
- A place in Tar-Baphon's future undead empire.

Radvir accepted.

---

# Radvir's Mission

Radvir returned to Caliphas with Bloodbrew and began secretly carrying out Adivion's plan.

His objectives were to:

- Assassinate influential elder vampires.
- Rise through vampire society.
- Frame others for the murders.
- Radicalize younger vampires using Bloodbrew.
- Build a new vampire army loyal to the Whispering Way.
- Eventually overthrow Luvick himself.

To conceal his actions, Radvir forged evidence blaming:

- Human nobles of Caliphas.
- The ancient nosferatu **Ramoska Arkminos**.

Meanwhile, every murdered elder increased Radvir's influence within the ruling council.

---

# The Witches' True Goal

Although they aid Adivion, **Aisa and Hetna** ultimately serve their own agenda.

Their former mentor was **Oothi**, a powerful annis hag.

When Oothi was sentenced to execution, she escaped by transforming herself into countless spider swarms using powerful magic. Her flesh escaped, but her skeleton was scattered throughout Ustalav to prevent her return.

For years, the sisters have secretly recovered her bones.

They have reconstructed almost her entire skeleton.

Only **Oothi's skull** remains missing.

They believe it lies somewhere beneath the **Abbey of Sante-Lymirin**.

---

# The Abbey of Sante-Lymirin

The abbey is owned by **Countess Carmilla Caliphvaso**, an aging noblewoman obsessed with preserving her youth.

The witches convinced Carmilla they could perfect an **Elixir of Youth** using research involving:

- Sun Orchid Elixir
- Vampire humors (the supernatural fluids that preserve a vampire's immortality)

In exchange for continuing this research, Carmilla granted them exclusive use of the abbey.

---

# The Abbey's Hidden Purposes

While pretending to research eternal youth, the witches secretly use the abbey to:

1. Search for Oothi's missing skull.
2. Brew Bloodbrew for Adivion.
3. Dispose of Radvir's victims.

---

# The Murders

Whenever Radvir assassinates a vampire, he delivers the body to the witches.

They:

1. Drain every remaining fluid from the corpse.
2. Remove anything useful for their experiments.
3. Leave the body somewhere in Caliphas before sunrise.

Morning sunlight reduces the vampire corpses to ash.

As a result, both humans and vampires discover mysterious "murder scenes," creating fear, paranoia, and political instability throughout Caliphas.

Neither side realizes these killings are part of Adivion's larger conspiracy.

---

# Summary

Adivion Adrissant seeks to ensure Tar-Baphon's return by overthrowing the old vampire aristocracy from within. Using the addictive Bloodbrew created by Aisa and Hetna, he manipulates Radvir Giovanni into assassinating elder vampires and recruiting a younger generation loyal to the Whispering Way.

Meanwhile, the witches use the Abbey of Sante-Lymirin as a front for brewing Bloodbrew, disposing of Radvir's victims, and searching for the final missing piece needed to resurrect their true master—Oothi.

# Carrion Crown Poem:

```poem
Upon the ashen pathways tread
Softly, as the whispered dead.
As mortal flesh doth rot and fail
To leech and maggot, ebbing frail.
Unhallowed words cannot be spoken,
With whispered oath, death lies broken.
Shed fear, shed life, shed pain, shed time,
Eternity seized shall soon be thine.

First spirit torn from Grave-Lady's grasp
Be rent and sown as soured ash.
Soft the spiral song reverses,
Judgment lost, damnation surges.
Keeper of the damned's soul take,
With packlord's heart the beast shall wake
And flesh be wrought in disarray—
Stillborn cocoon, to blessed decay.

A hundred slain lie innocent,
Grind bone and marrow to cement.
Craft now a skull of splintered graves,
Unmake life, unmake the slave.
Where history churns dream to blister,
Necrophagous secrets whisper
Through chronicles of Raven's tongue—
A legacy of fear unspun.

Blood spilt atop the Iron Thorn
Invokes that which cannot be born.
Arise the Tyrant now unbound,
Bearer of the Carrion Crown.
```
